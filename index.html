Manchester United History

Manchester United Football Club is a professional football club based in Old Trafford, Greater Manchester, England. The club was formed in 1878 as Newton Heath LYR Football Club, and it changed its name to Manchester United in 1902. The club has a rich history and is one of the most successful clubs in English football.

Timeline

1878
Newton Heath LYR Football Club is formed.
1902
The club changes its name to Manchester United.
1908
Manchester United wins its first Football League title.
1958
The Munich air disaster occurs, killing 23 people including 8 Manchester United players.
1968
Manchester United wins its first European Cup, beating Benfica 4-1 in the final.
1992
Sir Alex Ferguson becomes manager of Manchester United.
1999
Manchester United wins the Treble - Premier League, FA Cup, and UEFA Champions League.
2013
Manchester United wins its 20th Premier League title, surpassing Liverpool's record of 18 titles.
Present day
Manchester United continues to compete in the Premier League and other competitions, with a dedicated fanbase around the world.
